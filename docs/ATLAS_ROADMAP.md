# Atlas Roadmap

## Roadmap Philosophy

Atlas should evolve through durable capability epochs rather than disconnected
feature lists.

The roadmap should prioritize trust, reasoning quality, user context, and
long-term usefulness. Atlas should not rush toward automation before it has
earned the right to influence decisions.

## Epoch I: Building Intelligence

Status: completed.

Epoch I established the first deterministic intelligence foundations:

- Company analysis.
- Scoring.
- Explanations.
- Portfolio intelligence.
- Comparison.
- Watchlists.
- Memory.
- Provider abstraction.
- Decision reasoning.
- Market regime and market health.
- Risk sizing.
- Themes.
- Intelligence and conversation orchestration.
- Monitoring.
- Economic signals.
- Reasoning synthesis.
- Investor profile and suitability.

The goal of this epoch was to prove that Atlas can reason modularly across
businesses, portfolios, markets, themes, and investor context.

## Epoch II: Building Trust

Status: current.

Epoch II focuses on making Atlas trustworthy.

Goals:

- Make every rating explainable.
- Improve evidence quality handling.
- Separate facts, assumptions, missing information, and uncertainty.
- Standardize Atlas language.
- Preserve decision reasoning through the Decision Journal.
- Create product principles and guardrails.
- Document Atlas' constitution, product philosophy, architecture, and roadmap.
- Ensure user-facing experiences avoid false certainty and unnecessary trading
  pressure.

This epoch should make Atlas feel dependable, coherent, and honest.

## Epoch III: Building Habits

Status: future.

Epoch III should help investors build repeatable habits.

Goals:

- Daily and weekly review loops.
- Better monitoring workflows.
- Decision review cycles.
- Portfolio alignment checks.
- Watchlist hygiene.
- Investor profile refresh prompts.
- Clearer workflows for studying ideas before acting.

This epoch should make Atlas part of the investor's normal rhythm without
encouraging overactivity.

## Epoch IV: Building Wisdom

Status: future.

Epoch IV should help Atlas and the user learn from accumulated history.

Goals:

- Better analysis of decision quality over time.
- Pattern recognition across prior decisions.
- Improved investor-specific context.
- More useful lessons from decision journals.
- More nuanced confidence calibration.
- Better distinction between outcome luck and process quality.

This epoch should help the investor become better, not merely better informed.

## Long-Term Direction

Atlas should become a long-term investment operating system.

It should combine structured data, evidence quality, reasoning, suitability,
portfolio context, market context, investor memory, and calm language into one
coherent experience.

Atlas should grow slowly enough to remain trustworthy and deeply enough to
become genuinely useful.
